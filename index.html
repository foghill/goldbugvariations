POWERS, RICHARD · b. 1957

The Gold Bug Variations : a novel / by Richard Powers. — New York : William Morrow, 1991. — 639 p. ; 24 cm.

Time Magazine "Book of the Year" · National Book Critics Circle Award · "A formidable masterpiece" — Kirkus.

PROPERTY · BPL

Four interconnected characters — like the four molecules of DNA, and the four bass notes of Bach's Goldberg Variations — twist around each other in mirrored patterns as they try to decipher the complex codes of biology, of music, and of love.

— Directed by —

Mark A. Levinson

Director of Particle Fever, The Bit Player, The Universe in a Grain of Sand, Prisoner of Time — Stephen Hawking Medal for Science Communication · National Academies of Science Communication Award · Robert Sievers DaVinci Award. Former physicist. Experience on over forty-five feature films.

"I think films and formulas are both theories about how the universe operates."

— Based on the novel by —

Richard Powers

Won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction for The Overstory and the National Book Award for The Echo Maker. The Gold Bug Variations was named Time magazine's Book of the Year.

"Life consists of propositions about life."
